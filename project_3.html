    Embarking on a Pythonic Journey

    In 2017, during my high school years, I delved into learning Python, captivated by the idea of creating an AI assistant. This predates the AI surge of the 2020s, a time when I, like many inspired by Iron Man, sought to fashion a Jarvis equivalent. Initially dubbed LINK, as my creation was akin to the least intelligent neural network known. Link was a conglomeration of Google's TTS and STT APIs alongside Wolfram Alpha for math problems and Wikipedia for knowledge-based queries. Essentially, it was a rudimentary program reliant heavily on APIs.

    However, with the passage of time and the onset of the pandemic, I revisited LINK's development. Presently, it operates on Meta's Lamma LLM framework, with plans underway to transition it to Apple's latest LLM available on Hugging-face. The introduction of Apple's transformers ANE library empowered me to execute these LLM models locally on my MacBook. While the codebase undoubtedly requires optimization, LINK has officially earned it's title of a neural network based assistant for the first time this year.
