    LINK - A LLaMa 13B Based Assistant

    In 2017, I delved into learning Python captivated by the idea of creating an AI assistant. And so began my oldest project, LINK or the Least Intelligent Network Known.

    Initially LINK was just a conglomeration of Google's TTS and STT APIs alongside Wolfram Alpha for math problems and Wikipedia for knowledge-based queries. It was perfect for what it was designed to do, help me solve my homework so I can use my time more wisely by tinkering with my arduino.

    However, with the passage of time and the onset of the pandemic, I revisited LINK's development. After a couple of days of research I decided to actually make LINK what it was supposed to be, a neural network. I rebuilt it from the ground up to operate on Meta's LLaMa-2 7B framework, and the standard Transformers and PyTorch libraries. My discovery of Apple's transformers Apple's neural engine library hopefully will allow me to soon run the 13B model locally on my Macbook.
    I will be uploading my work on github as I rebuild LINK soon. In the meantime feel free to look at my other projects.
